-----------------------------Key Research Question-------------------- 
What are the major factors determining global petrol prices, and how do local policies and time-lags determine the transference of crude oil prices to the consumer level?

Fuel prices are a major driver of global inflation, and they directly affect the cost of living for billions of people. 
First and foremost, understanding if price fluctuations are a function of market fundamentals (crude oil), local institutional economic health (GDP/Exchange rates) or government policy intervention (subsidies), is critical for both members of the policy-making class and consumers to be able to forecast future trends in energy pricing.
I am trying to see and analyse what section drives the price of petrol at the pump area across various different regions. 
Moreover, I will regress the petrol prices against global energy benchmarks (Crude Oil) and macroeconomic factors (GDP Growth, Inflation, Exchange Rates). This is relevant because fuel prices drive inflation and heavily affect consumer purchasing power.
